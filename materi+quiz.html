METRIC: DIVIDING 🐱

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

Use GCF when you want to **split, share, or break** items down into smaller, equal groups. It guarantees that everyone gets the exact same amount with **no leftovers**!

🔑 GCF Secret Clues (Keywords):
• Greatest / Largest
• Maximum possible
• Divided / Shared equally
• No leftovers / Same amount
📊 Interactive Anchor Example:

Case: 12 Cat Toys & 18 Dog Treats. What is the maximum matching gift bags we can make?

Toys (Factors of 12): 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12 Treats (Factors of 18): 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18

👉 Greatest common factor is 6! So we make 6 identical bags (each has 2 toys & 3 treats).

⏰
METRIC: REPEATING 🐶

Least Common Multiple (LCM)

Use LCM when events **happen repeatedly at different intervals**, and you need to figure out when those events will line up or **coincide again in the future**.

🔑 LCM Secret Clues (Keywords):
• Smallest / Least / Minimum
• Next time / Again together
• Coincide / Repeat
• Cycles / Multiple intervals
📊 Interactive Anchor Example:

Case: Blue pet light flashes every 4s. Red light flashes every 6s. When do they flash together?

Blue (Multiples of 4): 4, 8, 12, 16, 20... Red (Multiples of 6): 6, 12, 18, 24, 30...

👉 Least common multiple is 12! So they will flash together again on the 12th second.
